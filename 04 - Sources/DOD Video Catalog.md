> [!NOTE]
> The 28 `DOD_111XXXXXX.mp4` files are the **DOW "Unresolved UAP Report" (`DOW-UAP-PR`) sensor videos** from the war.gov / PURSUE **Release 01** (8 May 2026) — the IR / electro-optical / full-motion-video accompaniment to the MISREP and Range-Fouler reports. Identified here from the official release inventory CSV (see [Source](#source)). The `DOD_111…` numbers are the war.gov CloudFront *filenames*; the release catalogs the same clips as **`DOW-UAP-PR19`–`PR49`**.

## What these are

Short sensor clips of UAP captured by U.S. military platforms (mostly infrared; some EO / SWIR / FMV), submitted by the combatant commands to [[AARO Overview|AARO]]. They span **2013–2026**, dominated by CENTCOM (Middle East / Iraq / Syria / Gulf), with EUCOM (Greece), AFRICOM (Djibouti), INDOPACOM (Japan / East China Sea), and a few domestic (NORTHCOM / Air Force / Army) entries. Each is the recording referenced by the redacted *"Video footage can be found at this link"* line in its mission report — and the release CSV **confirms this**: most pair directly to a `DoW-UAP-D` MISREP (the *"Paired report"* column below).

## The `DOD_` filenames

The Collection's 28 `DOD_111688723`–`DOD_111689759.mp4` are the CloudFront *filenames* for these same 28 clips. The release CSV keys the videos by **DVIDS ID + PR number**, not by the `DOD_` filename, so the exact `DOD_<id> ↔ PR##` per-file pairing is **not individually documented**. The IDs are sequential and almost certainly track PR order (so `DOD_111688723` ≈ PR19 … `DOD_111689759` ≈ PR49), but treat that per-file alignment as **inference**, not confirmed.

## Numbering observation

All 28 IDs fall in the narrow range 111688723–111689759 (a span of 1036) — consistent with a single batch upload, not 28 unrelated releases; some are adjacent (e.g. 111689082/083), suggesting cuts from the same source clip. The original hypothesis that *"this set is the bulk video accompaniment to the DOW MISREP and Range Fouler releases"* is now **confirmed** by the CSV's report-pairing column.

## Likely correlation with FBI Photo stills

The 32 FBI Photo series stills (A1–A8 PNG, B1–B24 PDF) are likely individual frames extracted from these videos — the visual style matches (targeting-pod IR imagery with central crosshair, hash-mark scale, redaction bars over sensor metadata). See [[FBI Photo Series Index]].
