## Narrative

While discussing the fragment field, the crew turned attention to a single distant bright object that was distinguishable from the nearby particles because it could be compared three-dimensionally against them. CMP Ron Evans:

> [!QUOTE]
> "Hey, Bob, I'm looking at what - what Jack was talking about; and it's definitely not a particle that's nearby because there is another one I can look at and get a three-dimensional comparison with. It is a - it is a bright object, and it's obviously rotating because it's flashing. It's way out in the distance ... about 10 or 12 Earth diameters."

The object was then attributed by the crew to the spent third stage:

> [!QUOTE]
> "That's the side and - of the S-IVB - and then the engine bell, Gene."

## Assessment

==Assessed conventional==. The flashing was interpreted by the crew as the rotation of the spent S-IVB stage, with sunlight glinting off the engine bell and SLA panels at a distance of roughly ten to twelve Earth diameters. The real-time three-dimensional comparison against the nearer fragment field is what let the crew separate this object from the local debris and identify it. Like the fragment field, this observation is periodically misrepresented in popular UAP discussion as anomalous despite an in-transcript conventional resolution.
