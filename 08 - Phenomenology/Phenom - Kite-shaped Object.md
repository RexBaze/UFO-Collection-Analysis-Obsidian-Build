> [!SUMMARY]
> A thin, kite- or diamond-shaped object bearing one red and one white light, seen at close range hovering near or above the ground; sometimes translucent. One of the four Western U.S. Event sub-incidents and a counterpart to the airborne "orb" phenomenology.

## Definition

A thin, dark or translucent kite/diamond-shaped object displaying one red light and one white light (the lights described as mismatched - "one red and round, one white and square"). Observed at close estimated range, hovering off-road or above the desert floor, moving silently at low speed (15-20 mph), leaving no dust and, in one account, a very thin (~4 ft) line on the ground. Some observers likened the ground-level light pair to a car or "Toyota Prius"; one reported seeing a star through the object (translucent).

## Conventional candidates

- A ground or low-altitude vehicle with mismatched lights, misjudged for shape in low light
- A drone with a kite-like frame and navigation lights
- Misperception of distance/size for unreferenced lights in darkness (AARO's stated baseline caution)

## Analytical note

Recorded on the Western U.S. Event notional map ([[Source - DOW-UAP-D078 Western US Event Notional Map]]) as Incident 3 ("Dark Kite") and Incident 4 ("Translucent Kite"). The "vehicle-mimicking" behavior - hovering off-road, no dust, evading on approach, leaving thin ground marks - recurs across multiple agent narratives and is the ground-level complement to the airborne [[Phenom - Orbs Launching Orbs|mother-orb]] signature from the same event.
