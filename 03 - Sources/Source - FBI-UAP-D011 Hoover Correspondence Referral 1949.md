## Summary

A historical 1949 Justice/FBI correspondence artifact, included as context rather than a UAP observation. It comprises a 31 January 1949 letter from Rev. Charles C. Barnes (Box 391, Oregon) to FBI Director J. Edgar Hoover reporting "four beams in the sky" converging over the Cascade Mountains the previous May, accompanied by small clouds and a "great explosion effect" at ~10,000 feet visible for 10+ minutes. Barnes speculated it related to pre-war European radio-beam rainfall experiments and Columbia River flooding, raising "national security." Page 3 is Hoover's brief February 1949 acknowledgment.

## Key extractions

> [!QUOTE]
> "a great explosion effect... a height of about ten thousand feet."

A routine public tip and courtesy FBI acknowledgment; pages 1 and 4 are handwritten/degraded OCR, the letter (p2) and reply (p3) legible.
