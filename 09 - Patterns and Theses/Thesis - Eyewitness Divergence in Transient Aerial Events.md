## Hypothesis

Multiple eyewitnesses to the same transient aerial event routinely produce materially different descriptions of size, shape, distance, behavior, and interpretation. This is consistent with the cognitive psychology and aviation safety literature on eyewitness reliability and ==does not require any unusual property of the observed object== to explain.

## Reasoning

1. The [[Incident - 2023-09 LiDAR Convoy]] is the cleanest example in this collection: two witnesses to the same event described it very differently (linear metallic gray object vs. distant point of light; close vs. far; structured vs. meteor-like).
2. The general aviation literature documents this pattern across decades of incident investigation.

## Confidence

## Implication

When evaluating any UAP incident with multiple witnesses, ==treat divergence as the default expectation==, not as evidence of cover-up or unreliability of any individual witness. Where witnesses converge on details, the convergence is informative; where they diverge, the divergence is informative about the difficulty of the observation, not necessarily about the object.
