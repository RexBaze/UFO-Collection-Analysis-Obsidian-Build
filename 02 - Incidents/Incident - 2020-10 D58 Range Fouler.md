## Assessment

==Unresolved with multiple anomalous attributes==. This is the only incident in the collection that combines:

1. ==Stable radar trackfile== (good return; rules out very low RCS)
2. ==Visual tally with deliberate-pattern lighting== (red blinking strobes - suggests intentional visual signaling, not natural)
3. ==Active noise jamming of the observer's radar== (suggests active electronic counter-measures by the objects themselves, or a third-party jammer providing cover)
4. ==Pair behavior with one circling the other== (cooperative or formation behavior)
5. ==Sub-frame disappearance (1/30 second)== (consistent with either departure faster than the F-16 sensor could track, or an instantaneous "drop" from sensor)
6. ==Metallic + reflective + balloon-shaped== (the only "balloon-shaped" descriptor in this collection paired with "metallic" - unusual combination)

Candidate explanations:

1. ==Coordinated foreign UAS with active EW package== (consistent with Iranian or Russian electronic warfare equipped drones; the balloon shape is unusual but could be a deceptive form factor)
2. ==Surveillance balloon plus accompanying drone== (the slow 20 kt drift fits balloon; the circling object could be an attendant drone; jamming could be a separate source)
3. Coordinated decoy / electronic warfare exercise that the aircrew was unaware of
4. ==Genuinely anomalous== (the combination of features is hard to explain by any single conventional candidate)

The 1/30-second disappearance is the most analytically significant single attribute: ==either the objects accelerated outside the sensor frame between two video frames, or they emitted/dissipated as point sources==. Both interpretations push the boundary of conventional explanations.
