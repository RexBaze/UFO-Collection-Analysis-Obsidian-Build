## Significance

Between 19 February and 25 March 1968, a CIA Information Report ([[Source - CIA-UAP-016 Himalayan Border Sightings 1968]]) tabulated repeated sightings of bright, fast-moving objects over the Sino-Indian/Nepalese frontier. Witnesses described rocket-like objects trailing white-yellow smoke at 20,000-25,000 ft, bluish high-speed objects bright enough to illuminate the ground, and accompanying thunder or blast sounds. Flight directions generally trended from the direction of Tibet (NE to SW). The report also relays an alleged recovery of a "huge metallic disc-shaped object" fragment in a crater at Baltichaur, ~5 miles NE of Pokhara, with similar pieces near Okhaldhunga.

The 1968 timing coincides with heightened Sino-Indian border tension and intensive U.S. interest in Chinese missile and nuclear activity, the lens through which the CIA filed these reports.
