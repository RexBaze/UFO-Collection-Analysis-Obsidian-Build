## Assessment

==Unresolved with strong contextual signal of active Iranian electronic warfare.== The UAP observation came roughly 3 hours after the second jamming event. The operational picture indicates:

1. The MQ-9 was operating well within Iranian air defense engagement range
2. Iranian air defense was actively contesting the aircraft (3 hails)
3. Iranian jamming (likely) succeeded in breaking C2 link twice
4. The aircraft was photographing Iranian SAM and naval assets
5. A UAP appeared on FMV at a coordinate near the disputed islands

Candidate explanations narrowed by context:

1. ==Iranian small UAS deployed in response to the MQ-9's surveillance== (most parsimonious given context)
2. Iranian missile, drone test, or air-launched munition
3. Larger Iranian UAS (Mohajer, Shahed-class)
4. Sensor artifact triggered by the same EW activity that broke C2 link (would be a stretch given the EO/IR sensor is largely independent of the SATCOM C2 link)
5. Unrelated atmospheric phenomenon

The combination of EW contest + UAP observation in the same mission is the strongest available indirect evidence that ==some 2020 Arabian Gulf cluster UAPs are Iranian UAS responding to U.S. ISR overflight==.
