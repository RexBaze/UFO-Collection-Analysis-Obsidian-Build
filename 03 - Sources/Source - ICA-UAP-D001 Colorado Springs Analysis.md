## Summary

An Intelligence Community Assessment of the 15 February 2022 sighting over Cheyenne Mountain by five U.S. Army service members at Fort Carson. The assessment's bottom line is that the object was "possibly backscattering of sunlight," held at low confidence. It reconstructs the solar geometry (sun ~27.5 deg elevation, ~125 deg azimuth at ~0945), notes 6-12 inches of snow on the mountain and conflicting cloud reports (witnesses said clear; AFWA and Weather Underground indicated partly-to-mostly cloudy), and proposes that sunlight backscattering off snow illuminated low altostratus cloud, then vanished when sun or cloud shifted.

## Key extractions

> [!QUOTE]
> "An Airborne Object Over Cheyenne Mountain in February 2022 was Possible Backscattering of Sunlight."

> [!QUOTE]
> "roughly the size of a large jet and resembling an angular, non-symmetrical potato made of uneven panels... completely stationary about 300-500 feet above Cheyenne Mountain while slowly changing shape."

> [!QUOTE]
> "No aircraft or balloons were noted active in or around Cheyenne Mountain during the time the witnesses saw the airborne object."

The assessment explicitly records "low confidence in this assessment based on uncertainty in the field of view of each witness, amount of snow cover, and exact elevation and amount of cloud cover."
