> [!SUMMARY]
> A classic structured disc: hollow-centered, with a ring of rotating lights on the underside that change color, capable of rapid vertical ascent. The signature of the 2008 Harare (Zimbabwe) airport sighting and, in spirit, of the historical 1948-1968 "flying disc" record.

## Definition

A disc-shaped object with a hollow center and a series of rotating lights on its underside. Lights shift color; "beams" were reported emanating from the object at one point. Reported hovering at high altitude before rapidly ascending out of visual range. Distinguished from the diffuse "orb" phenomenology by its reported solid structure and geometric detail.

## Analytical note

The Harare report ([[Source - CIA-UAP-017 Zimbabwe Harare Airport 2008]]) is notable for claimed dual-phenomenology detection (possibly radar plus optical) and for reaching the White House Situation Room. The structured-disc morphology connects the modern record to the historical 1948-1953 "flying disc/saucer" service studies ([[Source - DOW-UAP-D086 US Navy Flying Discs Report 1948]], [[Source - DOW-UAP-D084 US Army Flying Saucer Study 1949]]) and the 1968 Himalayan "metallic disc" report ([[Source - CIA-UAP-016 Himalayan Border Sightings 1968]]).
