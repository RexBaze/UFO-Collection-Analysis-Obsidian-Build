## Summary

Five first-hand narrative accounts (Narratives 1-5) from the federal agents who witnessed the October 2023 Western U.S. Event, supporting [[Source - DOW-UAP-D077 Western US Event AARO Analysis]]. All five independently describe bright orange "mother orbs" that hover silently and expel/"hatch" small red orbs (typically 3-4) into evenly-spaced lines, paired with horizon flashes, plus ground-level light objects that mimic vehicles, hover without raising dust, and evade on approach.

## Key extractions

> [!QUOTE]
> (D080) "too smooth and agile to be mechanical, but too structured/coordinated to be a biological division."

> [!QUOTE]
> (D081) red orbs expelled "like grapes being expelled from a basketball," no exhaust, no sound.

> [!QUOTE]
> (D079) of the translucent "vehicle": "I could see a star through the object."

> [!QUOTE]
> (D082) smaller lights appeared "as if being hatched from the larger very bright orange light."

Per-narrative notes: D079 (rangefinder-verified ~1,100 m mother orb; mismatched-light "Prius"-like vehicle, later translucent). D080 (most detailed; explicitly labels Incidents 1-3; high-speed ~70-80 mph pursuits; compares to "the Phoenix lights"). D081 (airfield "square" of four red lights; vehicle floats off-road leaving no tracks; "when the vehicle was gone, so were the orbs"). D082 (up to nine lights in a horizontal line above a ridgeline; object using terrain "to stay hidden"). D083 (numbered log with GPS coordinates; mismatched white-square/red-round light pair leaving a ~4 ft ground line; "cobweb"-like floating material).
