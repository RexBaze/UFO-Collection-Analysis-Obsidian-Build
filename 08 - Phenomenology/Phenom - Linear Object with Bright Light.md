## Definition

An elongated (linear, cigar, cylindrical) object with a dominant bright light source, often described as metallic or gray with the light on one end or side.

## Incidents

```dataview
LIST
FROM "02 - Incidents"

```

## Conventional candidates

- Aircraft with landing or anti-collision lights at angle that obscures wings (especially commercial aircraft in turn)
- Helicopters seen end-on with searchlight active
- Tethered aerostats
- Light from a known source (sun reflection, search aircraft, surveillance balloon) misinterpreted as the object itself
- Meteor or fireball seen end-on (the [[Source - FBI Serial 5 Redacted|Serial 5 witness]] explicitly proposed this)

## Analytical note

The [[Incident - 2023-09 LiDAR Convoy]] is the only well-documented linear-object observation in the modern portion of this collection. Witness disagreement within the same incident weakens the descriptor's reliability.
