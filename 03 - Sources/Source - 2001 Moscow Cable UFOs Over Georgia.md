> [!WARNING]
> ==This document is NOT a UAP report.== It is a State Department diplomatic cable about Russian airspace violations of Georgia. The "UFOs" in the title is sarcastic diplomatic euphemism used by a Russian MFA official. The cable was almost certainly captured into a UAP release set by a keyword sweep on "UFO."

## Summary

A Confidential cable from Ambassador Vershbow in Moscow to SECSTATE reporting that during a meeting with Russian Deputy FM Mamedov and MFA Georgia desk chief Tereoken, the Russians categorically denied any Russian aircraft violations of Georgian airspace or any Russian bombing of the Kodori Gorge in Abkhazia. Tereoken stated that "reports of planes in the area might as well have been about 'UFOs.'"

## Key extractions

> [!QUOTE]
> "ACCORDING TO TEREOKEN, THERE WERE CREDIBLE REPORTS OF ABKHAZ HELICOPTERS BOMBING THE AREAS 'WHERE THE TERRORISTS WERE' BUT THAT REPORTS OF PLANES IN THE AREA MIGHT AS WELL HAVE BEEN ABOUT 'UFOS.' MOSCOW, HE EXPLAINED, DOES NOT HAVE THE TECHNICAL CAPABILITY TO DETERMINE WHETHER THERE WERE FOREIGN PLANES IN THE REGION."

> [!QUOTE]
> "COMMENT: IT IS HARD TO ACCEPT OFFICIAL RUSSIAN DENIALS THAT RUSSIAN PLANES WERE NOT INVOLVED. TO POSIT THAT THEY COULD BE UFOS WOULD BE HUMOROUS IF IT WERE NOT FOR THE SERIOUSNESS OF THE VIOLATIONS."

## Significance

This document is the cleanest example in the collection of the ==keyword-sweep false positive== pattern. Anyone consuming AARO-era declassified release sets should not assume that every document tagged "UAP" is substantively about UAPs.
