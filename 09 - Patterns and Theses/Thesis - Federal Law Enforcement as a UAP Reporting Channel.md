## Hypothesis

Tranche 3 establishes ==U.S. federal law enforcement (the FBI and federal special agents) as a distinct, credible UAP reporting and investigative channel==, parallel to the military MISREP pipeline and feeding AARO - a channel that brings trained-observer ground reporting and direct agent observation into the corpus.

## Reasoning

1. The FBI generated formal records (FD-302 interviews, FD-1057 electronic communications) for the [[Incident - 2022-02-15 Colorado Springs Cheyenne Mountain|Colorado Springs]] and [[Incident - 2024 Northeastern US Plasma Orbs|Northeastern US]] cases, including a forensic-sketch unit reconstruction.
2. In the [[Incident - 2023-10 Western US Event Orbs Launching Orbs|Western U.S. Event]], the witnesses were six federal special agents whose accounts AARO rated favorably for credibility, and AARO issued a signed case memo.
3. FBI agents in the Northeastern case ==personally observed== the reported phenomenon on site, converting a civilian report into first-hand law-enforcement observation.

## Disconfirming evidence

- Law-enforcement observers are trained for terrestrial threats, not aerial-object identification; AARO itself flags optical-estimation limits in low light.
- No case in this channel yet includes instrument-grade data (radar, calibrated imagery); all rest on testimony plus, at most, blurry handheld photos.

## Confidence

confidence:: medium
basis:: A clear new institutional channel with credible observers, but still testimony-dominated and lacking technical corroboration.
