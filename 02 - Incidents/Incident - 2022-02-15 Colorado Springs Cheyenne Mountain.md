## Narrative

On the morning of 15 February 2022, five U.S. Army service members at Fort Carson observed a stationary object over the saddle of Cheyenne Mountain, roughly 6 miles to the west, for an estimated 2-5 minutes. They described a horizontal, off-white, non-metallic "bean/potato"-shaped object with a translucent shimmer and a surface of slowly shifting "fish-scale" panels. None had a phone to hand; the object vanished instantly when they looked away to retrieve one. Each independently sketched the object and the drawings agreed.

> [!QUOTE]
> "articulating fish scales or panels that were non-symmetrical, non-overlapping... each panel on the object shifted in slow waves." (FD-1057)

> [!QUOTE]
> "the object 'cloaked' in the space of time it took to turn a head and there was no shadow." (FD-1057)

## Assessment

==Inconclusive==. The ICA's bottom line is "possible backscattering of sunlight" off snow illuminating low altostratus cloud, but it explicitly carries this at low confidence, citing uncertainty in each witness's field of view, snow cover, and cloud cover. The central tension: a diffuse cloud-illumination effect does not naturally explain five trained observers' convergent description of a structured, paneled, shape-shifting object with defined edges, nor the instantaneous disappearance. The witnesses reported clear skies; the ICA relies on conflicting cloud reports. No aircraft or balloons were noted active in the area. Resolution would require the original sketches and a rigorous line-of-sight/solar-geometry reconstruction.
