## Narrative

The U.S. Embassy in Port Moresby received an informal inquiry on 28 January 1985 from the PNG National Intelligence Organization concerning sightings of "high-altitude, high-speed aircraft" over PNG on the evening of 24 January. Local residents in Wewak were frightened by overflights, prompting a public meeting attended by the Prime Minister, who was visiting his electoral district.

> [!QUOTE]
> "PILOT SAID HIS RADAR PICKED UP AIRCRAFT FLYING SOUTH TO NORTH AT HIGH ALTITUDE AND HIGH SPEED WHEN HE WAS OVER ANGORAM (VIC 4 DEG S, 144 DEG E). SEVERAL VISUAL SIGHTINGS OF CONTRAILS WERE ALSO REPORTED FROM VARIOUS POINTS AROUND PNG, OF ONE AIRCRAFT MOVING NORTH TO SOUTH AT 1900 LOCAL AND OF SIX-EIGHT AIRCRAFT TRAVELING SOUTH TO NORTH AT 2200 LOCAL."

The U.S. Embassy confirmed to the PNG NIO no B-52 overflights and no U.S. aircraft in PNG airspace on 24 January 1985, and requested confirmation and any additional information from USCINCPAC.

## Assessment

==Unresolved==. The most likely conventional explanation is military aircraft from an unnamed third country, but the State Department record contains no resolution. The pattern (multiple aircraft at high altitude in formation) is consistent with a long-range bomber or transport movement, not with the "small object" UAP profile that dominates the modern DOW collection.
