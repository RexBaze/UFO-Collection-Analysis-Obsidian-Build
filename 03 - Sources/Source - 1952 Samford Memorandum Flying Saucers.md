## Summary

A Secret-then-declassified internal State Department memorandum summarizing what Maj. Gen. John Samford, USAF Director of Intelligence (A-2), said about flying saucers at the Intelligence Advisory Committee meeting. The memo was generated because Paul Nitze had asked the author "what the Air Force thought about the flying saucers" and the author judged that other senior State officials would be interested.

## Key extractions

> [!QUOTE]
> "very little that can be said to clarify the flying saucer business. It is still a complete enigma. He did, however, point out that phenomena falling within the general description of flying saucers have actually been known to exist and have been reported one way or another for over 100 years."

> [!QUOTE]
> "There is no question, according to Samford, that ==credible observers are reporting the incredible.== The radar observation may have elements of 'electronic fluke,' but is sufficiently tied in with pilot observation so that it cannot be attributed entirely to this."

> [!QUOTE]
> "The Air Force views the flying saucers as a threat only because they are not understood and they are sufficiently frequent as a phenomena to mean that they will give great attention to them until they understand them."

> [!QUOTE]
> "General Samford did not say anything about the possibility that they were man-made or controlled, friend or foe, but the whole implication of his remarks was that this possibility was remote and not to be seriously considered."

## Notable

This 1952 internal Air Force position is functionally identical to the 2024 ODNI/AARO public posture: a real phenomenon, frequent enough to warrant institutional attention, with correlated radar and pilot observation, no demonstrated foreign or hostile origin, no resolved explanation. The historical continuity is striking.
