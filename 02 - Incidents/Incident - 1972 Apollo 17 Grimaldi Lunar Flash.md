## Narrative

LMP Jack Schmitt, observing the lunar surface during translunar coast:

> [!QUOTE]
> "Hey, I just saw a flash on the lunar surface! ... It was just out there north of Grimaldi. Just north of Grimaldi. You might see if you got anything on your seismometers, although a small impact probably would give a fair amount of visible light."

> [!QUOTE]
> "It was a bright little flash right out there near that crater. See the crater right at the edge of Grimaldi. Then there is another one north of it. Fairly sharp one north of it is where there was just a thin streak of light."

Capcom: "Okay. We'll check."

## Assessment

==Unresolved in the source document==. The most defensible interpretation (consistent with later TLP literature) is a small meteoroid impact on the lunar surface. Schmitt explicitly proposed exactly this hypothesis in real time and suggested the seismometers as a verification mechanism.

Whether the contemporaneous seismometer record contains a corresponding event is not in this collection.
