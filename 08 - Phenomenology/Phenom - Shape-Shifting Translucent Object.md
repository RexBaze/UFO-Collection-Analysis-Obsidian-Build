> [!SUMMARY]
> A stationary, white/off-white "bean" or "potato"-shaped object with a translucent shimmer and a surface of shifting articulating panels, which slowly changes shape and then abruptly "cloaks." The signature of the February 2022 Cheyenne Mountain sighting.

## Definition

An oval/bean/potato-shaped object, horizontal, matte off-white to opalescent and slightly translucent, displaying a "milky shimmer" that reflected sunlight. Its surface was described as articulating "fish-scale" or polygonal panels that shifted in slow waves while the object itself stayed perfectly motionless. Apparent size roughly that of a large jet or "10% of the mountain saddle." After 2-5 minutes it vanished instantly ("cloaked" in the time it took to turn one's head), leaving no shadow.

## Conventional candidates

- Backscattering of sunlight off snow illuminating low altostratus cloud (the ICA's low-confidence assessment; see [[Source - ICA-UAP-D001 Colorado Springs Analysis]])
- Lenticular cloud or other transient cloud formation
- Mylar/foil balloon catching sunlight
- Optical/atmospheric illusion (sun at ~27.5 deg elevation, snow on ground)

## Analytical note

The ICA assessed "possible backscattering of sunlight" at low confidence, explicitly citing uncertainty in each witness's field of view, snow cover, and cloud cover. The five-witness convergence on a structured, paneled, shape-shifting object is harder to reconcile with a diffuse cloud-illumination cause, which is the central tension in this case. The rendering ([[Source - FBI-UAP-D003 Colorado Springs Digital Rendering 2022]]) depicts a scaly, milky, lozenge-shaped object.
