## What it is

The Kuznetsov Carrier Task Group (KCTG) was the Russian Navy surface action group built around the aircraft carrier Admiral Kuznetsov. From late 2016 the group deployed to the Eastern Mediterranean off the Syrian coast in support of Russian operations in Syria, conducting combat flight operations and missile activity in the area.

## Relevance to this collection

The KCTG is the in-document conventional explanation for the 2016-11-18 Latakia observation: a P-8A monitoring the task group observed a low, fast, sea-skimming object outbound from the KCTG that the mission commander assessed as standard KCTG missile activity rather than an anomaly. The group is part of the broader pattern captured in [[Thesis - Russian Activity Drives Mediterranean Observations]].

## Notes

In these documents "KCTG" denotes the Russian naval formation whose known activity (carrier air operations, escort-ship missile activity) accounts for several Eastern Mediterranean observations. The "unknown" in such cases is usually the precise launch point or weapon type, not the origin.
