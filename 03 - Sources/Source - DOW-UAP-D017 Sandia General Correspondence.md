## Summary

A 116-page file of 1949 U.S. Air Force general correspondence concerning Sandia Base, Albuquerque, New Mexico, generated by Detachment D, 1100th USAF Special Reporting Group. Most of the file concerns routine base-security inspections (guard orders, fencing, access controls). ==Crucially, part of the correspondence is associated with the "fireball of July 24, 1949,"== one of the green-fireball events over the New Mexico nuclear-weapons complex that drove contemporary Air Force and scientific concern (the Project Twinkle / Lincoln La Paz green-fireball investigations).

## Key extractions

> [!QUOTE]
> ASSOCIATED WITH THE FIREBALL OF JULY 24, 1949. A fireball was reported to have passed over the general neighborhood of [Sandia]... [reports filed] half hours after the fireball was seen.

> [!QUOTE]
> SUBJECT: Security Inspection... Guard Orders have been amended directing that the electric gate be closed between 1700 hours until 0700 hours... Locks have replaced the bolts in all inspection access gates in the outer fence.

## Notable

The Sandia/Los Alamos green-fireball reports of 1948-1949 are a foundational episode in early U.S. government UAP concern: unexplained luminous phenomena over the most sensitive nuclear sites in the country. This file ties routine base security to one such July 1949 fireball, situating the phenomenon inside the security apparatus rather than a public sighting channel.
