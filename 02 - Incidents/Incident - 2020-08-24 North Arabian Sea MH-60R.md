## Narrative

An O-3 pilot from [[Unit - HSM-73]] flying side number 705 (BUNO 168122) on a night Surface Search Coordination (SSC) mission observed three unidentified small air contacts in the [[Location - North Arabian Sea]] at 0004:30Z. The pilot recorded:

- Negative electronic support (ES) indication
- No radar track
- No IFF response
- Unknown range and speed
- Apparent westerly heading

> [!QUOTE]
> "Initially observed 1x unknown air contact and tracked it before losing sight as it went behind a cloud. When contact on the unknown air contact was regained, 2x additional unknown air contacts were seen due east of the location of the initial contact. All 3x unknown air contacts appeared to maintain their relative course, speed, and altitude."

Shape characteristics noted: ==Round==, with ==Wings/Airframe==. (These boxes were both checked, suggesting either an ambiguous shape or a winged airframe with a rounded planform.)

## Assessment

The 3 contacts are ==unresolved==. The pattern (one contact, lost behind cloud, then reacquired with two additional contacts) is consistent with either:

1. Multiple objects already present, only some visible due to lighting and cloud cover
2. A single object that briefly multiplied (sensor artifact or visual misidentification)
3. A loose formation of small unmanned platforms with low radar cross-section

Given the date (August 2020), location (North Arabian Sea, near Iranian operating areas), and apparent westerly heading toward the Iranian coast, [[Thesis - 2020 Arabian Gulf Cluster|Iranian small-UAS introduction]] is a candidate hypothesis that the report cannot rule in or out.
