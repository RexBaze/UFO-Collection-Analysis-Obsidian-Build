## PURSUE releases (WAR.GOV/UFO)

The **Presidential Unsealing and Reporting System for UAP Encounters (PURSUE)** is the rolling public-release program launched 8 May 2026 on WAR.GOV/UFO, directed by the President and overseen by the Department of War with ODNI support. It releases "unresolved cases" in tranches every few weeks. Unlike the MDR batches above (which are declassification-review actions), PURSUE is the public-facing publication channel.

| Release | Date | Notable content |
| ------- | ---- | --------------- |
| Release 01 (tranche 1) | 2026-05-08 | First declassified photos, videos, and redacted reports |
| Release 02 (tranche 2) | 2026-05-22 | Additional photos, videos, and reports |
| Release 03 (tranche 3) | 2026-06-12 | 53 documents, 10 images, 6 videos: CIA UFO series (CIA-UAP-002-019), FBI domestic cases (Colorado Springs 2022; Northeastern orbs 2024-2026), the Western U.S. Event (AARO), 1948-1953 Army/Navy/USAF service studies, NASA Gemini debriefings, and a 1998 Congressional file |

Release 03 introduced new originating agencies to this collection: **CIA**, **ICA** (Intelligence Community Assessment), **AARO** as document author (not just receiver), the historical **Army / Navy / USAF** services, and **USG** (Congressional / White House) correspondence. Reported public interest: WAR.GOV/UFO had received over 1.7 billion hits worldwide since launch.

## Release process notes

1. ==Tearlines are the public-facing slice== of an underlying classified MISREP. The tearline is approved at UNCLASSIFIED while the underlying record may remain SECRET//NOFORN.
2. The MDR process is initiated by either a Congressional request, an internal review schedule, or an external request (typically not by individual FOIA but by aggregate AARO collection requirement).
3. Redaction patterns differ by exemption: ==(b)(1) 1.4(a)== blacks out national-security operational detail; ==(b)(6)== and ==10 U.S.C. §130b== black out personnel-identifying detail.
4. The release authority signature visible at the top of declassified pages is the final accountable official; the underlying review may have involved many cleared reviewers.
