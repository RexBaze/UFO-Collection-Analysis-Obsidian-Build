## Significance

Site of the October 2023 "Western U.S. Event," a two-day sequence of UAP observations by six federal law-enforcement special agents near a sensitive national security site. The exact location is withheld; the AARO analysis confirms U.S. military aircraft equipped with infrared countermeasure flares were operating in the airspace as part of a standard exercise, and that the terrain includes valleys, ridgelines, and an airfield. The notional map ([[Source - DOW-UAP-D078 Western US Event Notional Map]]) renders four sub-incidents across desert/basin terrain.

This is the highest-profile domestic case in the collection: AARO Director Jon Kosloski signed a formal case-analysis memorandum (5 June 2026) leaving roughly 40 percent of the reported phenomena unresolved.
