> ==Not a sighting.== D48 is a 181-page Research Triangle Institute technical report, 'Modeling Unlikely Space-Booster Failures in Risk Calculations' (10 Sep 1996), prepared for the USAF 30th and 45th Space Wing Safety Offices. It catalogs Atlas, Delta, and Titan launch-failure histories and classifies anomalous vehicle behavior into defined failure-response modes. Included as a ==misidentification-context document==: booster failures, staging events, and reentry debris are common terrestrial sources of unusual aerial phenomena that are later reported as UAP.

## Narrative

This document is a launch-safety engineering study, not a UAP observation. From the abstract:

> [!QUOTE]
> Missile and space-vehicle performance histories contain many examples of failures... In program DAMP, such failures are referred to as Mode-5 failure responses... This report shows how impacts from [these] failures are modeled... [with] a listing and brief narrative failure history of the Atlas, Delta, and Titan missile and space-vehicle launches.

Its value to the collection is contextual: it documents how often space boosters fail and behave anomalously, which is directly relevant to attributing some 'UAP' reports to staging, debris, fuel venting, or reentry.
