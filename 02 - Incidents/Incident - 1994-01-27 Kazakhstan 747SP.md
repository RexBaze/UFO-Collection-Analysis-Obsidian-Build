## Narrative

Tajik Air Chief Pilot Captain Ed Rhodes (American citizen) and his two American pilot colleagues (all former PanAm pilots) reported on 29 January 1994 that on 27 January 1994 they had encountered a UFO while flying their Boeing 747SP at 41,000 feet over Kazakhstan (~45 N, 55 E).

> [!QUOTE]
> "THEY FIRST ENCOUNTERED THE OBJECT AS A BRIGHT LIGHT OF ENORMOUS INTENSITY, APPROACHING THEM FROM OVER THE HORIZON TO THE EAST AT A GREAT RATE OF SPEED AND AT A MUCH HIGHER ALTITUDE THAN THEIR OWN. THEY WATCHED THE OBJECT FOR SOME FORTY MINUTES AS IT MANEUVERED IN CIRCLES, CORKSCREWS AND MADE 90-DEGREE TURNS AT RAPID RATES OF SPEED AND UNDER VERY HIGH G'S."

Captain Rhodes took photographs with a pocket Olympus camera and was to send copies to the Embassy and the Tajikistan desk if they came out.

> [!QUOTE]
> "AS IT WAS DARK WHEN THE OBJECT WAS OBSERVED, THE CREW WERE UNABLE TO DISCERN ITS SHAPE. THEY DESCRIBED THE LIGHT IT EMITTED AS HAVING A 'BOW WAVE' AND AS RESEMBLING A HIGH-SPEED PHOTO OF A BULLET IN FLIGHT, IN WHICH A VERY SMALL OBJECT GIVES OFF A MUCH LARGER TRAILING WAVE OF HEAT/LIGHT."

==Forty-five minutes after the initial sighting, at sunrise, the same aircraft flew UNDER the contrails the object had left behind.== Plane was making over 500 kts. Rhodes estimated the contrail altitude at approximately 100,000 feet, noting that "there is too little air/moisture at that extreme altitude to enable the creation of contrails by the propulsion mechanisms of ordinary aircraft which might be able to reach that height. The paths of the contrails reflected the maneuvers of the object, i.e., circles, corkscrews, etc."

The Embassy proposed a meteor or atmospheric-skip explanation. The crew, with extensive PanAm experience observing "thousands of falling stars," ==adamantly rejected the meteor hypothesis==. Captain Rhodes stated his opinion (apparently supported by his crew) that the object was extraterrestrial and under intelligent control.

Embassy comment: "WE HAVE NO OPINION AND REPORT THE ABOVE FOR WHAT IT MAY BE WORTH."

## Assessment

==Unresolved==. This observation has unusual evidentiary strength for its era:

- ==Multiple trained observers== (three commercial pilots, all with significant flight hours)
- ==Long duration== (40 minutes, far longer than the typical seconds-to-minutes UAP observation)
- ==Physical residue== (the contrails persisted long enough to be observed by the same aircraft 45 minutes later from below)
- ==Photographic record== (whether any photos survived is not in this collection)
- ==Witness sophistication== (the crew themselves proposed and rejected the meteor hypothesis from experience)

Plausible alternative explanations to consider:

1. Soviet or Russian high-altitude reconnaissance aircraft (the era and location are consistent; some Soviet aircraft did fly above 80,000 feet, and the post-Soviet successor states had reduced control over what flew where in 1994)
2. Re-entering satellite or upper stage (would not maneuver in circles or corkscrews)
3. Atmospheric phenomenon (none known to produce visible contrails at 100,000 ft with reflective maneuver patterns)
4. Hoax (low probability given the trained crew and the formal diplomatic channel)

This incident is the strongest "extraordinary maneuver and altitude" observation in this collection. It warrants further investigation if other source material (photos, contemporaneous radar data, Russian or Kazakh records) can be located.
