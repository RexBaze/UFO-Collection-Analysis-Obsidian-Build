> [!NOTE]
> Six Apollo-era still photographs released as visual material in the NASA-UAP series. Each has a yellow-highlighted inset calling attention to a small feature in the sky above the lunar horizon. Provenance is consistent with NASA Apollo Image Library frames re-released as part of an AARO-era curated visual material set.

## Assessment

The insets show features that are consistent with several mundane causes:

1. ==Lens flare or internal optical reflections== (especially VM3, where the sun is just below the lunar horizon producing visible solar corona, a textbook scenario for internal lens reflections)
2. ==Film grain or scan-time noise==
3. ==Hot pixels in the digitization scan== of original 1969-72 film
4. ==Stars or the orbiting Command Service Module== visible as point sources (possible but uncommon at typical Hasselblad film exposures)

==None of the insets in this collection provides shape, motion, or sensor data sufficient to support an interpretation beyond "small bright/colored feature against dark sky."== Without the original film negative, exposure metadata, and sensor calibration, the insets are inconclusive.

The Apollo Image Library at NASA assigns AS12- and AS17- frame numbers to all original Hasselblad frames; matching these AS numbers against the curated VM filenames would reveal the original mission frame metadata.
