> [!SUMMARY]
> A roughly meter-scale red or yellow sphere with a bright white "plasma" core, observed at close range and low altitude in the Northeastern U.S. case. Often appears in pairs that move in tandem and merge.

## Definition

A red (or yellow) ball of light, described as basketball- to beach-ball-sized at close range or "~1 meter" with a basketball-sized white "plasma sun" at its center. Reported at 20-30 ft altitude and as close as 20-30 yards from observers. Characteristically silent, smooth-moving, sometimes appearing in identical pairs that travel in formation and then merge into a single object before disappearing. Witnesses report no eye strain or immediate health effects, though the primary witness alleges correlated gamma-radiation and electronics anomalies.

## Conventional candidates

- Ball lightning or plasma discharge (rare, short-lived; inconsistent with repeated multi-year recurrence)
- Drones with illuminated payloads (witness and agents argued treeline density made drone flight implausible)
- Chinese lanterns / pyrotechnics / aircraft lights (do not account for the merging behavior or close range)
- Camera/long-exposure artifacts (several FBI photos were blurry long exposures; some "orbs" may be optical)

## Analytical note

This signature differs from the [[Phenom - Orbs Launching Orbs|Western U.S. "mother orb"]] in scale (close and low vs. distant and high) and in the merging rather than launching behavior, but both involve silent red spheres. The strongest evidentiary element is that FBI agents personally observed pulsating white/red lights on site in November 2024. The claimed radiation and electronics effects are unverified. See [[Source - FBI-UAP-D009-D010 Northeastern US 2026 Orb Interviews]].
