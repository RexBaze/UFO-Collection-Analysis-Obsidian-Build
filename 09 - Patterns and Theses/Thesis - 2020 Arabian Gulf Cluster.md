## Hypothesis

The 2020 cluster of unresolved UAP observations in the [[Location - Arabian Gulf]] and adjacent [[Location - North Arabian Sea]] is best explained by a combination of (a) increased U.S. ISR coverage and standardized reporting after Navy 2019 reporting guidance, and (b) the operational debut of small Iranian unmanned aerial systems and IRGC Navy unmanned platforms in the region.

## Reasoning

1. The Arabian Gulf has hosted persistent U.S. Navy and Air Force presence for decades, but the 2020 calendar year is the first in which mandatory UAP reporting under the 2019 Navy guidance produced a documentary record at scale.
2. Iranian small UAS development matured through the late 2010s; multiple types had operational debut in this period.
3. The phenomenological profile of the observations (small, low radar cross-section, varied speeds from 40 to 500 kts, often without IFF) is consistent with small unmanned platforms.
4. ==Counterpoint==: a single-event observation pattern of objects that hold formation and altitude without thermal exhaust signature is harder to attribute to known Iranian UAS, which tend to have detectable IR signatures.

## Disconfirming evidence

- Some 2020 reports describe objects with no thermal exhaust profile, which is more consistent with passive aircraft (gliders, balloons) than with powered drones.
- Iranian small UAS in 2020 were not generally known for the maneuvers reported (sudden acceleration and direction change).
