## Narrative

Between 19 February and 25 March 1968, a CIA Information Report tabulated repeated sightings of bright, fast objects over South Ladakh, NE Nepal, North Sikkim, and Western Bhutan. Witnesses described rocket-like objects trailing white-yellow smoke at 20,000-25,000 ft, bluish high-speed objects bright enough to light the ground, and accompanying thunder/blast sounds, generally moving from the direction of Tibet (NE to SW). The report also relays an alleged recovery of a "huge metallic disc-shaped object" fragment in a crater at Baltichaur, ~5 miles NE of Pokhara, with similar pieces near Okhaldhunga.

> [!QUOTE]
> "A huge metallic disc-shaped object... was found in a crater at Baltichaur, five miles NE of Pokhara."

## Assessment

==Unresolved==, with strong conventional candidates. The 1968 timing and NE-to-SW (Tibet-origin) trajectories are consistent with Chinese missile/rocket testing and re-entry debris, which is precisely the intelligence concern that prompted the CIA to collect these reports. The "metallic disc fragment" claim is uncorroborated and characteristic of period rumor. Retained as an incident for its historical weight and its place in the disc-morphology lineage, while flagging missile/re-entry activity as the leading conventional explanation.
