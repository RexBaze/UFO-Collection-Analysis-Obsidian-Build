## Definition

Brief, localized changes in the appearance of the lunar surface observed from Earth or from lunar orbit. Most are now attributed to small meteoroid impacts (which liberate brief flashes detectable by sensitive instruments), to outgassing events along fault lines, or to observer effects (atmospheric distortion, eye fatigue, instrument artifacts).

## Incidents

- [[Incident - 1972 Apollo 17 Grimaldi Lunar Flash]]

## Significance

The Apollo 17 Schmitt observation is consistent with the modern understanding of TLPs (small meteoroid impact most likely). The location (near Grimaldi) is on the lunar near side and has been the subject of historical telescopic TLP reports. NASA's Lunar Impact Monitoring program (2005-present) has documented many such flashes; the Apollo 17 transcript captured one in real time from a vantage point closer than Earth-based telescopes.
