## What AARO is

The All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office is the Department of Defense office responsible for detecting, identifying, and attributing objects of interest in, on, or near the surface of Earth across all domains (air, sea, space, and trans-medium). Established in 2022, AARO succeeded earlier task-force structures including the UAP Task Force.

## Why it matters for this collection

AARO is the central receiving authority for the modern declassified DOW material in this collection. Every DOW MISREP, Range Fouler Debrief, and INDOPACOM tearline in the post-2022 portion of the corpus has been routed through AARO as part of either Congressional reporting or public release obligations.

## Mandate elements relevant to evaluation

1. ==Detection== through coordinated multi-platform sensor tasking
2. ==Identification== using all-source intelligence
3. ==Attribution== to known classes of objects when possible (foreign UAS, balloons, sensor artifacts, natural phenomena)
4. ==Anomaly resolution== where conventional attribution fails
5. ==Reporting== to Congress (annual)

## What AARO does and does not do

AARO does:

- Receive and aggregate UAP reports across military services and AORs
- Conduct technical analysis using military and IC sensor capabilities
- Coordinate declassification reviews for public release
- Publish annual reports

AARO does not:

- Publicly disclose individual MISREPs in full
- Issue independent classified findings to the public
- Operate its own primary sensor network (uses service-provided sensors)

## Tranche 3 expansion (2026)

PURSUE Release 03 (12 Jun 2026) shows AARO acting as a document **author**, not just a receiver: AARO Director **Jon Kosloski** signed the formal case-analysis memorandum for the [[Incident - 2023-10 Western US Event Orbs Launching Orbs|Western U.S. Event]] (DOW-UAP-D077, 5 Jun 2026), which leaves ~40 percent of the reported phenomena unresolved. Tranche 3 also brings domestic (NORTHCOM-AOR) cases and a federal-law-enforcement reporting channel into AARO's remit, alongside the historical CIA/Army/Navy/USAF record. See [[Thesis - Federal Law Enforcement as a UAP Reporting Channel]] and [[Thesis - Domestic CONUS UAP Reporting Emerges 2021-2026]].
