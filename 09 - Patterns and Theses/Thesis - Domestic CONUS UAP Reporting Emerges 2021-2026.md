## Hypothesis

The PURSUE Release 03 (tranche 3) marks a structural shift in this collection: ==the center of gravity moves from overseas military mission reports to domestic (NORTHCOM-AOR) UAP cases reported by U.S. service members, federal agents, and civilians between 2021 and 2026==, opening a new line of evidence distinct from the CENTCOM corpus.

## Reasoning

1. Before tranche 3, the corpus was ~70% CENTCOM and entirely overseas/military or NASA/historical. Tranche 3 adds four domestic incidents in two years ([[Incident - 2022-02-15 Colorado Springs Cheyenne Mountain]], [[Incident - 2023-10 Western US Event Orbs Launching Orbs]], [[Incident - 2024 Northeastern US Plasma Orbs]], [[Incident - 2026 Northeastern US Red Orb Sighting]]).
2. These cases share new phenomenology absent from the CENTCOM record: orbs "launching" orbs, red plasma-core spheres that merge, shape-shifting translucent objects, and kite-shaped ground objects.
3. They originate through new channels - FBI field offices and an AARO formal case memo - rather than through MISREP/tearline pipelines.

## Disconfirming evidence

- Domestic reporting may simply reflect a release/declassification choice (PURSUE surfacing FBI files) rather than a real change in where phenomena occur.
- The 2023-09 [[Incident - 2023-09 LiDAR Convoy|LiDAR Convoy]] case shows at least one CONUS domestic case predates tranche 3, so the shift is one of volume and emphasis, not a clean first appearance.

## Confidence

confidence:: medium
basis:: Four new domestic incidents with shared, novel phenomenology, but the trend may be an artifact of what was declassified rather than of underlying event distribution.
