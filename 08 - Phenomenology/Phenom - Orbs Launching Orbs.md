> [!SUMMARY]
> A luminous orange "mother orb" that appears briefly and expels a cluster of smaller red "orbs," which then fly off in evenly-spaced formation. The signature of the October 2023 Western U.S. Event and the dominant new phenomenology introduced in tranche 3.

## Definition

A large bright orange or amber spherical light that appears for one to two seconds and releases (or "launches"/"hatches") two to four smaller red spherical lights, repeatedly over a period of hours. The red orbs adopt horizontal or vertical formation, show varied and seemingly coordinated kinematics, occasionally loiter (in at least one case for several hours over a ridgeline), and are described as silent. The pattern's defining feature is its repetition.

## Conventional candidates

- Military aircraft dispensing infrared countermeasure flares (AARO's strongest partial explanation; flares were confirmed active in the airspace, plausibly accounting for ~60 percent of reported activity)
- Misidentified military aircraft exhaust (ruled out by AARO; altitudes too high, inconsistent with silence and loitering)
- Multi-rotor UAVs (unlikely; multi-hour loiter exceeds battery limits)
- Celestial/atmospheric misperception (unlikely given multiple synchronized viewing angles)

## Analytical note

AARO judged roughly 40 percent of the reported phenomena unexplained after first-stage analysis, with the multi-hour stationary red "orb" cited as physically incompatible with any known flare burn-time. The "launching" morphology has no clean conventional analogue and is the single most evidentially interesting behavior in the domestic tranche 3 corpus. See [[Source - DOW-UAP-D077 Western US Event AARO Analysis]] and [[Phenom - Kite-shaped Object]] for the related ground-level objects from the same event.
