## Narrative

A [[Platform - P-8A Poseidon]] from [[Unit - CTG 67.1]] monitoring [[Russian KCTG|Russian Kuznetsov Carrier Task Group]] activity in the [[Location - Eastern Mediterranean]] observed an unidentified low-flying object 55 NM northwest of Latakia, Syria from an unknown origin, traveling at approximately 500 kts on a southeasterly heading outbound from KCTG, for approximately 2 minutes. The object was detected via the EO/IR sensor in apparent sea-skim mode and was observed to pass between the Russian INGUL ARS (Auxiliary Replenishment Ship) and one unidentified vessel.

> [!QUOTE]
> "The mission commander for P-8 characterized the interaction as safe. While this was the first observed occurrence of possible missile activity by P-8 aircraft in the Eastern Mediterranean it is assessed to be standard activity consistent with the assessed activity of the KCTG."

## Assessment

==Assessed conventional==. The in-document assessment is Russian KCTG missile activity, consistent with the carrier task group's 2016 deployment to support Russian operations in Syria. The "unknown" was the precise launch point and missile type, not the existence or origin of the launch.

This incident is instructive as a baseline for what a sensor-rich, intent-rich observation looks like when conventional attribution is straightforward. Compare with observations lacking sensor metadata or operational context.
