## Narrative

Over two days in October 2023, six federal law-enforcement special agents (three two-person teams) reported a recurring sequence near a sensitive national security site in the western United States: a luminous orange "mother orb" appeared for one to two seconds and expelled a cluster of two to four smaller red "orbs," which flew off in evenly-spaced formation, repeating over several hours and paired with horizon flashes. In at least one case a red orb remained stationary above a ridgeline for several hours. The agents also reported ground-level objects (the "Dark Kite" and "Translucent Kite") that mimicked vehicles, hovered without raising dust, and evaded on approach. The AARO notional map ([[Source - DOW-UAP-D078 Western US Event Notional Map]]) records four sub-incidents: Orbs Launching Orbs, Fiery Orb, Dark Kite, Translucent Kite.

> "a luminous orange 'mother orb' appeared to produce smaller red 'orbs,' one after another, multiple times over a period of several hours." (AARO, D077)

> [!QUOTE]
> red orbs were expelled "like grapes being expelled from a basketball." (Agent narrative, D081)

## Assessment

==Unresolved==. AARO (memo signed by Director Jon Kosloski, 5 June 2026) cross-correlated the accounts against military/commercial flight logs, radar, and ADS-B and evaluated seven hypotheses. Infrared countermeasure flares from confirmed military aircraft plausibly account for ~60 percent of the reported activity, but ~40 percent remains unexplained: for those events no known aircraft were in the observers' line-of-sight, and a multi-hour stationary red orb is "physically incompatible with the burn-time and descent rate of any known military flare." Exhaust, UAVs, foreign activity, and natural/celestial causes were ruled out or judged unlikely; "unrecognized technology" is a pending exclusion-based hypothesis for up to ~40 percent. No technical data (video, photo) was captured, which AARO flags as the key limitation.
